Complete Guide to HVAC Filter Sizes

HVAC filter sizes are defined by three dimensions: width x height x thickness, measured in inches. These are always nominal (rounded) dimensions — the actual filter is slightly smaller to allow easy installation. A complete residential HVAC system uses filters ranging from 10x20x1 in small units to 25x25x1 in large returns, with thickness options of 1, 2, 4, or 5 inches.

Non-Standard and Odd HVAC Filter Sizes

Approximately 15–20% of residential HVAC installations use non-standard filter sizes due to custom ductwork, retrofits, or regional variations in construction practices. Common odd sizes include 17x22x1, 19x21x1, 15x20x1, and 18x24x1. If your size is not on store shelves, here is your best option:

  • Custom filter fabricators — Local HVAC supply houses can often order custom-cut filters from regional manufacturers for large or unusual sizes.

What Happens When You Use the Wrong Size Filter

Using a filter that is too small is one of the most common HVAC mistakes. Even a 1/4-inch gap around the filter allows significant air bypass. Research has shown that an undersized filter allows 10–30% of airflow to bypass filtration entirely, effectively reducing a MERV 13 filter to MERV 4 performance for that portion of air.

Signs you are using the wrong size filter: (1) The filter moves or rattles when the system runs. (2) You can see light around the filter edges when installed. (3) Your filter never seems to get dirty even after months. (4) Your home seems dusty despite regular filter changes.

4-Inch Filter Sizes — The Media Cabinet Standard

When upgrading to a 4-inch media filter system, the nominal size works the same way — it is just 4 inches thick instead of 1 inch. The most common 4-inch media filter sizes are 20x25x4 (most common), 16x25x4, 20x20x4, and 16x20x4. Media cabinets are designed around these standard sizes, so any filter manufacturer's product in the correct nominal size will work in any brand's cabinet.

Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most common HVAC filter sizes?

The top 5 are: 20x25x1, 16x25x1, 20x20x1, 16x20x1, and 14x25x1. For 4-inch media filters, 20x25x4 is the most common.

Where can I buy non-standard HVAC filter sizes?

Nordic Pure, FilterBuy, and Second Nature offer custom-cut filters for non-standard sizes. Most ship within 3–5 business days.

What if my filter size is not available at Home Depot?

Home Depot stocks about 15–20 common sizes. For anything unusual, order online from Nordic Pure or FilterBuy — they offer hundreds of sizes.

Is a 16x20 the same as 20x16?

Yes — filter sizes are sometimes listed in different order. By convention, width x height x depth, but some retailers reverse width and height. A 16x20x1 and 20x16x1 are the same filter.

What is the thickest HVAC filter I can buy?

Standard media filters go up to 5 inches thick (e.g., 20x25x5). Some specialty systems use 6-inch filters. Your filter slot depth determines what you can install.

Can I stack two 1-inch filters to make a 2-inch?

Do not do this. Two stacked 1-inch filters dramatically increases static pressure, often causes filter collapse, and may not physically fit. Buy the correct thickness.

What size HVAC filter does a 3-ton AC unit use?

HVAC unit tonnage does not determine filter size. Filter size is determined by the size of the return air duct or air handler filter slot. Measure the existing filter.

How do I know if my filter is the right size?

The filter should slide in smoothly with no gaps around the edges. The airflow arrow should point toward the air handler. You should not be able to see light around the filter edges when installed.

Do apartments typically use standard filter sizes?

Most apartments use either 16x20x1 or 20x20x1 filters. Some older apartment buildings use 12x24x1 or other less common sizes. Always check the existing filter before ordering.
